                         C. Summary Description of Proposed Project [including Project structure, components, activities, technical design, flow of
                         funds, etc.]
                         The Project’s purpose is to expand Caixa’s housing construction projects with the “Selo Azul” certification (Portuguese for
                         “Blue Seal”) through providing of new credit line using blended finance through a combination of IBRD, IFC and Government
                         resources.
                         The Selo Casa Azul provides certification housing projects that meet certain social and sustainability standards, scored
                         based on criteria for: (i) urban quality and well-being (ii) energy efficiency and sustainable comfort, (iii) efficient water
                         management, (iv) sustainable development, (v) social and (vi) innovation.


                        DATE                                                                                 Official Use Only              Page 2 of 13

                         The blended finance will be under the umbrella of the “Eco Invest Brasil” program which is a Brazilian Government initiative
                         designed to attract foreign private investments essential for the country's ecological transformation. The program aims to
                         strengthen climate finance and create a favorable environment for sustainable investments in Brazil with by providing
                         blended finance using government resources to leverage international financing, which in this case is being provided by
                         IBRD and IFC.
                         Component 1: Financing for Certified Sustainable Housing Projects (USD198,000,000.00). This component will provide a
                         credit line for real estate projects that adopt certifications in their projects. The financial product will provide favorable
                         terms for these projects, taking advantage of the combination of the IFC, IBRD and Government resources through Eco-
                         Invest.
                         Subcomponent 1.1: Sustainable Housing Production. This subcomponent will provide a credit line for developers to housing
                         projects that have sustainability certifications.
                         Subcomponent 1.2: Sustainable Retrofit This subcomponent will finance the retrofit of housing projects that have
                         sustainability certifications. This credit line targets developers that would undertake retrofitting of existing building stock.
                         This market has challenges in terms of technical complexity, legal restrictions for urban and environmental licensing and
                         costs. This sub-component intends to address these deficiencies taking advantage of the blended finance and working with
                         Caixas recent efforts to expand this market.
                         Component 2: Technical Assistance (USD2,000,000). This component is designed to better equip Caixa to propose,
                         operate, and scale the innovations provided for in the credit line; and, provide "education" and "advocacy" to public and
                         private entities to support sustainable construction.




For Official Use Only
                         Subcomponent 2.1: Institutional Strengthening. This subcomponent will support training of CAIXA's technical staff on
                         sustainability and other innovations in the housing sector including (i) Development of a tool for environmental and social
                         monitoring and supporting the evaluation of the Program's results; (ii) Technical support the Caixa team in improving its
                         innovative housing portfolio products (e.g., environmentally certified buildings, modular construction; social housing
                         PPPs).

                         Subcomponent 2.2: Technical Assistance for Public and Private Agents. The resources allocated under this subcomponent
                         will support the training of public and private agents to facilitate sustainable/certified construction including: (i) the
                         development of practical guides and training for interested construction companies to qualify for the program and obtain
                         certification. (ii) development of advocacy materials and their dissemination; (iii) dissemination and outreach activities
                         with the public and private sector.

                         Brazil’s economy grew steadily, with GDP increasing by 3.2% in 2023 and maintaining a similar pace in 2024. Poverty rates
                         declined to 21.3%, supported by improved economic conditions, social protection programs, and a higher minimum wage.
                         Unemployment dropped to a historic low of 6.1%, and real household incomes rose. Although the pace of poverty reduction
                         slowed as social transfers stabilized, continued job growth and targeted social policies are expected to further reduce
                         poverty.

                         About 87% of Brazil’s population and 90% of its jobs are concentrated in urban areas. The construction sector employs over
                         3 million people and accounts for 8% of new job creation. Urban expansion—driven mainly by housing construction, which
                         has grown by 2.8% annually—has helped address the country’s housing deficit (6 million units in 2022), but also intensified
                         challenges such as inadequate housing, unmaintained buildings, and increased demand for infrastructure and services.

                         Urban growth has contributed to environmental pressures: by 2015, urban areas accounted for half of Brazil’s CO₂
                         emissions. Cities face significant climate risks, with an average of 2,740 climate-related events annually, leading to public
                         and private sector losses. These impacts are expected to worsen as temperatures rise and rainfall patterns become more
                         extreme, increasing flood risks and reducing water availability. The expansion of housing into flood-prone areas, along with
                         increased urban heat and congestion, further exacerbates these challenges.

                         Housing and construction activities drive up the consumption of materials, water, and energy—households account for
                         21% of electricity use and 68% of water consumption—and generate most of Brazil’s waste. Throughout their lifecycle,
                         housing was responsible for 9% of Brazil’s greenhouse gas emissions in 2013, mainly from energy use, material production,

                         E. 2. Client’s Organizational Capacity/Borrower’s Institutional Capacity
                         Caixa is a state-owned financial institution with over 26,500 service units in more than 99% of Brazilian municipalities and
                         86,962 employees (44.9% women), and has the capacity and commitment to the environmental and social management
                         of its risks. The National Monetary Council (CNM in Portuguese), a deliberative federal agency, has issued Resolution
                         4,943/2021 that mandates that financial institutions address social, environmental, and climate risks. Complying with
                         these requirements, Caixa has in place an Environmental and Social Management System endorsed by the institution’s
                         Senior Management. Caixa’s Social, Environmental and Climate Responsibility Policy (PRSAC) incorporates five pillars: a)
                         management of social, environmental and climate risks; b) management of administrative and supply chain practices for
                         governance; c) disclosure and reporting of information; d) promotion of sustainable development; and e) relationship and
                         engagement with stakeholders. Since 2009 Caixa adheres to the Equator Principles and applies IFC’s Performance
                         Standards for loans that pose significant E&S risks.

                         Caixa’s structure includes a national managerial unit, the Gerência Nacional de Riscos Especiais, responsible for daily E&S
                         risk management, including screening, identifying, assessing risks, and monitoring borrowers' compliance with required
                         measures. Also, Caixa established a Sustainability Committee in 2022 and a Vice-Presidency of Sustainability and Digital
                         Citizenship in 2023. Annually, Caixa discloses information through the Sustainability Report and the Social,
                         Environmental, and Climate Risks and Opportunities Report, aligned with CNM’s resolution requirements.

                          II. SCREENING OF POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL (ES) RISKS AND IMPACTS

                          Environmental and Social Categorization - CATEGORY A, B, C; FI-1, FI-2 and FI-3
                         This transaction is provisionally categorized as FI-2. The project is categorized as FI-2 in accordance with OP/BP 4.03.
                         According to the World Bank’s BP 4.03, an FI-2 category is appropriate when adverse environmental and social risks
                         and impacts arising from most of the activities are expected to be few in number, generally site-specific, largely
                         reversible, and can be readily addressed through mitigation measures. The main E&S risks of this operation are
                         associated to Caixa’s ability to apply the newly created screening procedures (under the newly created E&S screening
                         tool) and manage sub-projects, which are expected to be of a medium E&S risk nature and potentially associated to
                         Occupational Health and Safety and impacts on communities and the environment. The criteria outlined in Caixa’s
                         subproject screening tool developed for the two other WB operations serve to classify the environmental and social
                         risks associated with each proposal. These include factors such as large-scale resettlement or expropriation, impacts
                         on indigenous and quilombola lands, adverse effects on vulnerable populations or cultural heritage, occupational and
                         community health and safety risks, adequacy of risk management and staff training, environmental licensing




For Official Use Only
                         requirements, suppression of native vegetation, noise, and changes in soil, water, and air quality, as well as
                         greenhouse gas emissions and climate-related vulnerabilities. Regulatory compliance regarding environmental
                         authorizations and licenses is also considered. During preparation, the tool will be adapted to this operation,
                         considering the Selo Caixa Azul E&S requirements. Subprojects that are classified as having high or substantial risk will
                         not be eligible for this project.
                         Also, a subproject is eligible for the Selo Azul certification if it complies with the specific requirements of the Selo Azul,
                         meets the prerequisites of CAIXA’s programs, fulfills the mandatory criteria, and achieves the minimum score in the
                         sustainability categories, with all requirements verified through technical documentation and reports.
                         The process involves document analysis, inspections, and confirmation of the implementation of the proposed
                         sustainable solutions. Examples of criteria include: urban quality, such as adequate infrastructure, relationship with

                         harvesting; sustainable production, including waste management, use of certified wood, industrialized components,

                          Sexual Exploitation and Abuse/Sexual Harassment (SEA/SH) Risk Rating

                         The SEA/SH risk rating is low. Brazil has a strong legislation on SEA/SH, domestic violence and violence against women
                         and children. SEA/SH are defined as crimes in the Penal Code (Art. 216-A, 215-A, 218-B, and 228) and Law 13,431/2014. A
                         robust network of referral services to SEA/SH and GBV survivors is broadly available, follows a survivor-centered
                         approach, offers social, psychological, pedagogical, and legal care and guidance, pursues criminal action, and provides
                         protective measures. Despite this strong legal framework, the World Bank GBV Profile for Brazil (June 2023) highlights
                         that the country has one of the highest rates of GBV globally and there are significant challenges in the enforcement and
                         implementation of these laws, resulting in low execution by the government. However, it is important to contextualize




For Official Use Only
                         this information, as the predominant form of gender-based violence in Brazil happens intradomicile, which is not directly
                         relevant to WB projects that primarily assess risks of project-related SEA/SH.

                         Following CMN’s Resolution 4,943/2021, one of the risks that require assessment refers to acts of harassment,
                         discrimination or prejudice based on personal attributes, such as ethnicity, race, color, socioeconomic status, family
                         status, nationality, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, religion, belief, disability, or health and ideological or
                         political positioning. Caixa has a Code of Conduct and a Policy to Prevent and Combat Moral and Sexual Harassment and
                         Discrimination. Its employees must annually sign a term of acknowledgement of the Code of Conduct. Caixa’s channels
                         for receiving reports of possible infringements of the Code of Conduct also address SEA/SH incidents. They prohibit
                         retaliation against their users and allow anonymous registration. The specific procedures requested to Caixa's borrowers
                         in this Project will be assessed during Project preparation.

                        Other Relevant Project Risks

                         Overview of all potential risks of relevance for the project that may impact the environmental and social assessment:
                         The environmental and social risk Moderate (Category FI-2), mainly due to civil works. These activities are situated in
                         urbanized, modified environments and are not expected to cause habitat loss or significant impacts on natural habitats.
                         Typical civil work impacts include waste generation, machinery emissions, dust, and hydrocarbon leaks. Occupational
                         health and safety risks involve construction hazards such as working at heights, electrical shocks, noise exposure,
                         ergonomic risks, stress, and fatigue. These risks and impacts are expected to be site-specific, temporary, reversible,
                         and manageable through standard GIIP practices as outlined in the WBG's EHSGs and the Environmental and Social
                         Management Policy and ESMS of Caixa. The Project is not expected to cause significant permanent impacts; on the
                         contrary, the adoption of sustainable practices—such as energy efficiency and the use of low-carbon materials—
                         reduces emissions throughout the lifecycle of the buildings. Certified projects prioritize the use of devices and
                         technologies that reduce energy consumption, such as natural lighting, cross ventilation, solar heating, and renewable
                         energy generation. The implementation of systems for water savings, individual metering, greywater reuse, rainwater
                         harvesting, and permeable areas for infiltration is also promoted.

                         The subprojects are expected to be implemented in consolidated urban areas, which reduces the likelihood of

                         and safety (OHS) for workers involved in construction and retrofitting activities, as well as potential temporary
                         disturbances to neighboring communities during civil works. There is also a risk of insufficient inclusion of
