                                          I.       THE PROPOSAL

1.     I submit for your approval the following report and recommendation on (i) a proposed project
loan, and (ii) a proposed results-based loan, to the People’s Republic of China (PRC) for the
Heilongjiang Green Transformation Demonstration Project and Program.

2.      The proposed Heilongjiang Green Transformation Demonstration Project and Program
will assist Heilongjiang to promote sustainable, climate-resilient, low-carbon, and green
development in the cities of Heihe and Suifenhe adopting a comprehensive approach targeting
the main sources of environmental pollution and greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, as well as
environmental restoration. 1 This would be achieved by (i) supporting river and wetland restoration,
sponge cities, flood protection, climate-resilient infrastructure, smart city systems, and capacity
building; (ii) improving operations and energy efficiency of port and logistics facilities, economic
and free trade zones (FTZs), and institutional capacity to support sustainable supply chain
logistics; and (iii) supporting the government plans to improve the energy efficiency of buildings.
The project and program will promote regional public goods by reversing the negative impacts of
pollution within and beyond the PRC and play a demonstrative role for municipalities with
pervasive ecological pressures, and leverage counterpart financing from the Heihe Municipal
Government (HMG) and Suifenhe City Government (SCG) of Heilongjiang Province.

                     II.      THE PROJECT AND ITS PROGRAM COMPONENT

A.      Rationale

3.      Heilongjiang is in the northeast PRC, with a population of 38 million in an area of 454,000
square kilometers. The region was one of the main heavy industrial and mining bases during 1950–
1980, which incurred economic losses after reforms were implemented in the 1990s. The PRC
State Council has initiated a strategy for revitalizing the industrial base and focusing on the
development of an environment-friendly and climate-resilient economy, with an increased focus
on education, health care, and cultural projects.2 As of 2020, Heilongjiang has legacy environmental
degradation and remains one of the poorest provinces in the PRC, with gross domestic product per
capita that ranks 30th out of 31 provinces.3

4.      The project and program area includes Aihui District of Heihe Municipality (0.19 million
population, including 50.5% females) and Suifenhe City (0.07 million population, including 50.1%
females).4 Heihe is in the northern part of Heilongjiang Province and lies on the western side of
Heilongjiang River. Heihe is considered a key node under the national strategy of revitalizing the
PRC’s northeast industrial bases. Suifenhe is in southeast Heilongjiang Province with two class I
inland ports (highway and railway) on the border. It is a key inland-port city and a main trade channel.
Heihe and Suifenhe were chosen to host two of six new FTZs identified by the national government
in 2019 to diversify Heilongjiang’s economy.5 They face several development challenges including
aging urban infrastructure, poor connectivity and logistics practices, climate risks, water and air
pollution, limited knowledge of green technologies, and absence of a coordinated multisector

pollution as trade and industry play a dominant economic role in the two cities.

5.       River ecological pollution, climate adaptation, and mitigation. The existing sewerage
systems in the project and program area were designed as combined stormwater and sewerage
systems, and were not systematically planned, nor designed to cope with the pace of urbanization.
As a result, sewerage overflows occur during storm season (June to September), with sewage
directly discharged into streams and waterways. This leads to seasonal fluctuation of water quality
in the river system, from class III to class IV or V.6 Heihe and Suifenhe cities face significant
climate risks, including extreme rainfall and storm events, pluvial and fluvial flooding, and extreme
low temperatures. Climate impacts are exacerbated by inadequate and poorly maintained
infrastructure. The frequency and severity of extreme weather events, including storms, floods,
extreme cold and hot temperatures, and drought are projected to increase, leading to
environmental degradation, negative impacts on infrastructure, and decreased urban well-being.
The project and program will (i) address these challenges by supporting river and wetland
restoration, sponge cities, flood protection, climate-resilient infrastructure, and smart city systems
for climate risk management; and (ii) support the PRC in implementing its nationally appropriate
mitigation actions and nationally determined contributions (NDCs) by delivering nonmotorized
transport (NMT), energy efficiency upgrades, and increased urban green space.

6.      Supply chain management. The provincial government prioritizes logistics and supply
chain management improvement in the 14th Five-Year Plan.7 Heihe and Suifenhe are important
nodes for supply chains that deal with agricultural products, textiles, petroleum, timber, steel,
manufacturing parts, and manufactured goods. Heihe river port is currently located downtown,
which compounds congestion and increases pollution. The current facilities and logistics services
are inefficient, polluting and have been strained by the health and quarantine requirements of the
coronavirus disease (COVID-19) pandemic. The project will develop a new green river port in
Heihe and improve logistics efficiency in Heihe and Suifenhe.

7.      Regional environment improvement. Industrial development is located along riverbanks
and in the city centers, which has polluted the urban environment and water sources. The
pollutants eventually flow into international river basins, and on to the Pacific Ocean. To mitigate
this pollution, enhance livability, and drive more sustainable economic development, the project
will support the government to develop well-connected economic and FTZs based on green
building principles and to rehabilitate several river sections. This will improve regional public
goods, particularly improved water quality and discharge into the Pacific Ocean.

8.       Tourism. Rural tourism is developing rapidly in Heihe due of its unique geographical
location, natural scenery, and folk culture. Suifenhe is also a popular medical tourism destination.
However, tourism growth is challenged by (i) aging infrastructure; (ii) COVID-19 pandemic; and
(iii) lack of institutional and human capacity to promote sustainable tourism, and investment
capital. Women have limited opportunities for employment in the sector. The project will develop
infrastructure and institutional capacity to support sustainable and community-based tourism with
a focus on women-oriented capacity building and training.

9.      Smart city development. In 2019, Heihe established a data center, and in 2020, began
to develop the Heihe Phase 1 Smart City program to improve citizen- and business-oriented
online services and urban management. However, the current platforms only allow simple data
analytics, with limited ability to derive information to inform urban management and planning.

Improvements and additional features are needed to (i) generate insights on the urban environment,
(ii) promote efficiencies, and (iii) bring more government services online. The project will develop
Smart City Phase 2, improving integration of local and provincial institutions responsible for
environment, forestry, district heating, water resources, early flood warning, disaster response (fire
and flood), and logistics and port management. The emphasis will be on functionalities that
improve the efficiency, sustainability, and climate resilience of Heihe, improve coordination across
sectors, enable informed decision-making, and enhance public service delivery.

10.      Energy-intensive building sector. The building sector is responsible for 40% of global
energy-related annual GHG emissions.8 The PRC ranks second globally for energy consumed by
the building sector, and first for energy consumed by residential buildings. To cope with cold
winters and the harsh climate, buildings in Heilongjiang require significant heating, which is highly-
energy intensive; however, the region has been slow to adopt green building standards for new
building development. The project will support the use of international green building standards
including passive building design, water-saving features, and intelligent building energy and
management systems. This will demonstrate best practice in sustainable building construction
and management and support the PRC in achieving its NDCs by promoting significant (up to 60%)
energy savings in the building sector compared with conventionally constructed buildings. Many
existing, older residential buildings in the region are energy-intensive and were built to poor
insulation standards and need refurbishment to reduce energy losses.9 To ensure a system wide
approach to reducing emissions in the building sector, a results-based loan (RBL) component (para.
12) will be included to retrofit old buildings.

11.     Institutional Strengthening. Heilongjiang’s focus on an environment-friendly and
climate-resilient economy requires comprehensive cross-sector planning, interagency
coordination and alignment with national priorities and objectives. National objectives devolve to
the provincial level but need to be implemented at the county and city level. Implementation
requires developing competent institutions, strategies, and plans. For example, in the transport
sector, lack of an NMT network plan, which is uncoordinated with other civic infrastructure in
Suifenhe and Heihe, constrains the decarbonization of the transport sector, and achievement of
a cleaner and safer urban environment. The project will develop a NMT masterplan for each city,
identify strategies for implementing the highest priority corridors and projects and provide key
policies and safety measures. The project will support the development of green port investment
plans that include replacement of diesel-powered port handling and other equipment with electric
and non-fossil energy equipment, and introduction of smart port systems for reduction of
congestion and emissions at port gates.10 Further, interagency coordination between local and
provincial institutions, which is not in place needs to be established and enhanced. Improved
coordination across sectors, would enable informed decision-making, and enhance public service
delivery.

14.      Lending modality. HPG requested a regular investment loan project combined with an
RBL program. The combination of the investment loan and RBL will result in a better outcome by
greening and reducing emissions from new and existing infrastructure. The investment loan will
focus on efficient and climate-resilient infrastructure, restoring environmentally degraded areas,
green ports and facilities, and application of international green building standards for new
buildings (para. 10). This is best suited to an investment loan project approach as the
implementing agencies lack experience in these innovative approaches. The RBL program would
complement this by demonstrating an effective model to refurbish existing residential buildings in
extreme cold zones to improve energy efficiency and reduce GHG emissions. RBL is the most
suitable modality given the many small and dispersed activities for which a transaction or input-
based approach as under investment lending would not be efficient or as effective in terms of
sustainable program results. ADB’s support for the program and the strong government
ownership will enhance the implementation of the medium- and long-term government plans. It
will demonstrate best practices for replication in similar programs in other extreme cold zones in
the PRC and other ADB developing member countries through ADB’s planned knowledge sharing
activities. 18 ADB’s support will enable the program to be expanded, thereby accelerating the
overall climate mitigation and urban environmental improvement benefits. The individual contracts
for building renovation will be small in value and are appropriate for the RBL modality. Further,
the RBL modality will have lower transaction costs for ADB and the government.

(i) integrated, multisector solutions for climate change adaptation and disaster resilience are
important to improve livability of urban areas and reduce GHG emissions; and (ii) the use of
technology, such as pipeline leak detection system, require parallel institutional strengthening and
improved governance to improve outcomes, such as water resource management.19 The project
and program will draw on successful innovations from ADB-financed projects elsewhere in the PRC,
including incorporating the international road assessment program (iRAP) safety assessments to
improve safety for all road user types, 20 and the use of sponge city 21 infrastructure for flood

C.        Project and Program Description

16.     The project and program are aligned with the following impact: environmental
improvement in Heilongjiang achieved (footnote 17). The project and program will have the
following outcome: environmentally sustainable transportation and urban and regional
development enhanced. The project and program will have five outputs. Detailed descriptions are
included in the design and monitoring framework (Appendix 1), project administration manual (PAM),
and program implementation document (PID).24

17.      Output 1: Infrastructure and services to enhance climate resilience and livability of

B.       Economic and Financial Viability

30.       An economic analysis was prepared following ADB guidelines. Benefits include (i) energy
conservation and pollutant emission reduction through renovation, (ii) residents’ non-use values
accrue and revealed preference for recreational and tourism facilities, (iii) disability adjusted life
years saved through sewerage service expansion, (iv) vehicle operating costs and value of time
saved through improved public transport and supporting transport in the new port, (v) land value
savings in FTZ and standard workshop, and (vi) storage cost savings through capacity expansion
in logistic facilities. The economic internal rate of return for the project and program were
calculated at 15.9%, which surpasses the 9.0% economic opportunity cost of capital. Economic
viabilities were confirmed under various sensitivity scenarios. 41 The economic internal rate of
return for the five outputs is 15.4% to 17.2%. Additional unquantified benefits include regional
public goods (e.g., improved water quality in river basins, and biodiversity through wetland
restoration). Improvements to logistics facilities and the port will enhance trade efficiency.

31.       A financial analysis was conducted to assess the financial viability of revenue generating
subprojects and financial sustainability of nonrevenue generating subprojects. Two revenue
generating subprojects—the standardized workshop, and new port—are financially viable, with a
financial internal rate of return (FIRR) of 6.32% and 6.31% that exceeds their weighted average cost
of capital of 3.96% and 2.27%. Sensitivity analysis indicates that the subprojects are financially viable
under the following scenarios: 10% capital cost increase,10% operating cost increase, 10% revenue
decrease, and 1-year benefit lag. The urban wastewater treatment subproject has a negative FIRR
of -3.76% and the FTZ development subproject has FIRR of 4.83%, which is less than its weighted
average cost of capital of 6.02%, indicating that two revenue generating subprojects cannot recover
their full costs, but the revenue generated fully covers the operation and maintenance (O&M) costs.
Counterpart funds will be provided by Heihe Qiaotou District Construction and Development Co.,
Ltd. and Heilongjiang Suidong Pilot Zone Development and Operation (Group) Co., Ltd. The
companies have the capacity to repay debt service for the whole project and to cover O&M costs
of nonrevenue generating components during the operation period. While the companies lack the
capacity to provide sufficient counterpart funds during the construction period, Heihe and
Suifenhe governments have committed to providing the remaining funds.42
