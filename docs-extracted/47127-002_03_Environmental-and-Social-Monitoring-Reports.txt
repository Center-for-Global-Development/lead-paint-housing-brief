1        INTRODUCTION & PROJECT OVERVIEW
1.1      Purpose of Report
The purpose of this report is to provide the ADB, AFD, MCDC and the MRG with updated
information on the progress of implementation and the level of compliance of all environmental
and social activities carried out by PMO, the PISC and the Construction Contractors during
MUSIP 1 implementation. These activities refer to (i) Environment, Social, Health and Safety
(ESHS) issues associated to construction activities, (ii) Involuntary Resettlement (IR),
(iii) Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM), (iv) Gender Equality and Social Inclusion (GESI).

The level of compliance of the activities performed is appreciated by reference to the
concerned requirements expressed in the contractual reference documentation including:
     Loan Agreement Covenants for all E&S obligations of the Borrower;
     Project Administration Manual (PAM) particularly by reference to its sections VII
      (Safeguards), VIII (Gender and Social dimensions) and IX (Performance Monitoring,
      Evaluation, Reporting and Communication);
     Initial Environmental Examination (IEE), Resettlement Plan (RP) and Gender and Social
      Inclusion Action Plan (GESIAP) Reports as approved by ADB and MONREC/EDC for all
      activities under the responsibility of MCDC-PMO and the Construction Contractor;
     Detailed Environment, Social, Health and Safety (ESHS) specifications as presented in
      the bidding documentation (Section 6 – Employer’s Requirements) for all aspects related
      to Construction Contractor responsibilities;

The ADB supported components started in 2017 (the Loan effective date is April 19, 2017).
The AFD supported contract started mid-January 2018.

By the time of the preparation of the present report ESSMR No5, construction activity started
in December 2018 on Shwe Ge Pumping Station site is still on-going. It is the unique
construction site under activity in November 2019.

1.2      Project Overview
1.2.1 General Information
The Mandalay Urban Services Improvement Project (MUSIP) is supported in its first phase
(MUSIP1) by a range of loans and grants managed by the two co-financiers, ADB and AFD.
The project has three outputs: (i) improved water supply systems, (ii) improved wastewater
and drainage management, and (iii) strengthened capacity for urban services management.

1.3.3 Progress of Shwe Ge Construction Works during reporting Period
The PMO continued to hold the position of supervision Engineer until 29th May. In June Temporary
Works were 5 months in delay and Permanent Works 3 months in delay based on original programme.
The main reasons were commercial dominance, and inexperienced staff leading to abortive work. The
Contractor continued to ignore contract procedures and requirements throughout the entire period.
During the second part of the report period, there were no Permanent work activities carried out by the
Contractor, who also did not provide the planned design update and cancelled the designer mission.
The Contractor did not install an effective dewatering system; groundwater and surface water was not
controlled within the Site area. There was no improvement on safety during July and August and the
Engineer therefore issued two Notices to Correct – slow progress and ESHS (unsafe working conditions,
failure to protect and stabilise excavation at the outlet dyke area, incomplete and uncompacted
Cofferdam, no presence of safety manager/engr/inspector, environmental monitoring, sediment
management plan, non-compliance on worker camp and incomplete ESHS document) in August..
The Engineer and PMO issued numerous reminders to follow up submissions, rectifications, etc. for the
same recurring items in meetings and letters but there was no action or improvement by Contractor.
The Employer wrote to the Contractor’s head office on 18th August instructing replacement of the
Contractor Representative and safety officer.
Internal Employer-ADB-PISC discussions, and draft notice letters were prepared in order to terminate
the works contract due to the inept performance of the contractor. The JV head offices were called to
meetings with the Employer on 23rd and 29th August, during which the dire Site situation was discussed.
The Contractor’s management stated the project will be in delay and promised to finish the whole work
by end January 2020.The Employer granted the Contractor a two weeks grace period to demonstrate
improvement in performance and subsequently decided not to terminate the works contract.
At the end of September sinkholes developed and the stability of the works slopes and the road dyke
were brought into question. The entrainment of fine particles in the inadequately screened dewatering
process was considered to be the cause. Numerous discussions between Contractor and Engineer
civil and geotechnical engineers took place throughout October. The dyke was subject to geophysical
tests (ground radar and shear wave) and remedial work suggestions made however the Contractor
was reluctant to engage specialist companies (dewatering).
Reinforcement fabrication for the rc base slab of the PS was underway in October. The Time for
Completion expired on 21 October. At the end of October preparatory works (excavation, cofferdam,
access) were some 90% complete. No structural concrete had been placed. The only Permanent Works
were the stone columns soil reinforcement which could be considered as some 25% of the works in
terms of contractor’s programmed time. The stone columns represent 9% of the total contract amount,
and 15% of the value of the Permanent work. 18% of the overall contract amount had been certified to
date. A realistic achievable updated construction programme has not been submitted.

Regular inspection is conducted every Friday by PMO-ESHS Team since February 2019

audited the last week of October by the International and National ESHS PISC specialist
accompanied by PMO ESHS staff.

3.2      Public Consultation and Information
No particular public consultation was carried out by the PMO-PISC ESHS Team during
the period May 2019 to October 2019.

The GSD team requested a consultation budget from the Project Steering Committee
and organized a Project Orientation Session (POS) on August 1 st, 2017 in a ward in
Thingazar Creek area to field test the Orientation Material. A Project Orientation Session
Budget was approved in September 2017. Subsequently the team developed a Project
Orientation Session Concept Plan to guide the remainder of the Orientation Sessions,
including women only meeting in all project townships.

One POS was carried out by PMO-PISC GSD Team, with the support of PMO ESHS
Engineer in November 2017 and in the communities of the proposed Solid Waste Pilot
Reception Facility (MYWW/1.2, recently cancelled by MCDC). Public consultation of
communities close to Nankad and Shwe Ge Pumping station were carried out by PMO
in Nge Toe village on 19-20 June 2018.

Since the paper on Nankad WWTP component released in the Friday Times on June 17,
2017, limited press or media release is observed until end of October 2019: Article about
MUSIP in MCDC Annual Magazine and articles reported by MCDC in 2 newspapers.

Two information booklets were completed (ShweGe and Thingazar), a third one is under
completion (Nankad WWTP). The project includes the procurement for an International
Knowledge Product Specialist (MYWW/45.0) and a National Knowledge Product
Specialist (MYWW/46.0) to prepare communication material as video. Mission was
completed end of May 2019 and the video is available.

5        CONCLUSIONS , PRIORITY ISSUES AND ACTIONS
Detailed ESHS specifications were provided in the bidding documents in Volume 2
(Employer’s Requirements) Part 1 (General Technical Specifications for the Works)
Section 2.5 (Environmental, Social, Health and Safety). These specifications, which
detail measures proposed in IEE-EMP, concern the obligations of the Contractor
regarding ESHS documentation, technical measures (waste and wastewater
management, air and water monitoring, sediment control, etc.), training, occupational
health and safety, recruitment and labour management, worker camps.

Shwe Ge is the first construction contract signed under the MUSIP1. As an evidence, the
selected contractor (Chinese Joint Venture) neglects deliberately most of its obligations
regarding ESHS. The previous ESHS audit (May 2019) identified not less than 94 non-
compliances for 138 ERs ESHS criteria audited, or about 68.6% of criteria not satisfied.
Some limited improvements observed during present ESHS audit reduce the score to 44
non-compliances for 113 ERs ESHS criteria audited or 38.9%.

The main breaches with Loan Agreement and Covenants obligations observed still
include (i) the start of construction works by the CC without prior approval by PMO of
Construction Contractor ESHSMP, (2) the non-compliance of the CC with Employer’s
ESHS Requirements for several indicators, (3) the absence of external monitoring to be
appointed by PMO, (4) the non-compliance of CC with ER’s regarding labour
employment conditions, (5) still the absence of a MUSIP Project website to be opened
by MCDC.

Concerning IEE-EMP obligations, the breaches observed address the non-respect of
ESHS construction obligations defined in the Contractor’s Contract.

Key non-compliances identified during the construction site ESHS audit include:
    Non-compliant CC-ESHS documentation, not yet approved by PMO while
     construction works are on-going since almost a year;
    Still the recruitment of most of the local workers on a daily basis without a working
     contract, which is forbidden by ERs;
    No recruitment medical check-up,
    No free access to medical care during construction,
    Uncertainties regarding insurance coverage of daily basis workers in case of
     occupational accident,
    No significant training delivered.

Situation is expected to improve with the works supervision being now under the
responsibility of the PISC and its permanent representative on the Shwe Ge construction
site.

The following urgent actions are expected from the CC:

    To improve First Aid Kit content on site in compliance with OSHA ANSI/ Z308.1-

     line in the middle of the shared lane may help the drivers to better stay in their “half
     lane”.

Concerning IRC and GRM, activities are well advanced. Compensation for land
acquisition is completed with the exception of one owner requesting land for land
compensation instead of cash. It is understood from PMO that the replacement land,
next to Shwe Ge, is presently a part of the lake next to the road, which will be reclaimed
at the end of Shwe Ge construction. This area is Government land. MGR approved in a
letter dated 10 June 2019 received by MCDC the transfer of the replacement land to the
concerned owner. Three letterboxes for the reception of grievances are still installed in
main Project centres by PMO.

Regarding GSD activities, the GESIAP Stakeholder Communication performance
measurement and data recording responsibility matrix has been completed. Preparation
for and implementation of Project Orientations Sessions (POS) was carried out up to
December 2017, with the community located close to the Solid Waste Pilot Reception
Facility, by PMO GSD staff with the assistance of GSD National specialist. The PMO
ESHS Engineer participated in the POS in order to deliver a simple presentation of
measures considered to reduce potential nuisances during construction stage. However,
no specific GESIAP activities have been recorded during the present reporting period,
which is not compliant with the obligations stated in the Loan Agreement.

Among the next tasks to be undertaken by PMO:
    Strengthening of ESHS staff, still limited to 3 persons but not dedicated exclusively
     to MUSIP as it should be.
    Development of MCDC-MUSIP 1 website.
    Contracting of an External Monitoring Agency (EMA) to carry out verification
     monitoring of activities related to resettlement and GRM, in compliance with PAM
     requirements.
    Implementing, through its dedicated staff, activities in accordance with those listed
     in the GESIAP. Performance criteria on the subject will be provided in the next
     ESSMR.
