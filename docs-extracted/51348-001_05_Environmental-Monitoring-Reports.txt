                                          I.      THE PROPOSAL

1.    I submit for your approval the following report and recommendation on a proposed loan to
the Republic of Uzbekistan for the additional financing of the Mortgage Market Sector
Development Program.1

2.       The Asian Development Bank (ADB) approved the program on 28 November 2019.2 It
was designed to increase the availability of affordable market-based residential mortgage credit
by establishing the premise for improved access to long-term market-based funding and
enhanced development of competitive housing finance products, as well as reforming the
inefficient housing subsidy framework and some of its programs. The program’s impact is living
standards improved for the population of Uzbekistan. The program has three outputs (para. 6).
The additional financing will expand the scope of output 3 to include supplementary long-term
funding to the Mortgage Refinancing Company of Uzbekistan (UzMRC) to make long-term local
currency resources available to identified eligible participating financial institutions (PFIs) to fund
(i) residential mortgage loans, (ii) housing improvement loans, and (iii) green renovation loans (a
new loan product). Further improvements are also supported, including the establishment of an
information and technology system in UzMRC, enhanced gender access to UzMRC funding, the
operationalization of the green renovation loan tool kit, and UzMRC’s adoption of a strategic
business plan.3

                                          II.      THE PROGRAM

A.      Rationale

3.     The program is performing well based on the following assessment. The proposed project
meets the eligibility criteria for additional financing.
       (i)     Program performance rating. The program’s performance has been rated
               on track in eOperations since concept approval. This on track rating is expected to
               continue until the program’s closing dates. As of 15 July 2022, $167.65 million has
               been disbursed—$25 million under the policy-based loan (PBL) tranche 1 and
               $142.65 million under the financial intermediation loan (FIL). Of the $142.65
               million, 70.67% or $100.81 million has been liquidated. The remaining $7.35
               million under the FIL and the PBL tranche 2 of $25 million will be disbursed in
               October 2022.
       (ii)    Delivery of expected outputs. The program is rated successful. It is on track to
               meet all its outputs and outcomes.
       (iii)   Safeguard compliance. The program has complied with ADB’s Safeguard Policy
               Statement (2009). It is categorized FI-C for all three safeguard categories
               (environment, involuntary resettlement, and indigenous peoples) and is rated
               on track across all three safeguards categories in eOperations.
       (iv)    Management of risks. The program’s risk management is rated successful. Risks
               were identified during the program processing. No major risks or integrity concerns
               have materialized during implementation.

4.       Given the good progress under output 3 (para. 7), the government requested ADB support
to scale up the FIL component of the program—bringing significant improvements by providing
additional long-term funding for UzMRC to make long-term local currency resources available to
identified and eligible PFIs. The additional financing is consistent with the government’s National
Development Strategy, 2022–2026 4 and ADB’s country partnership strategy for Uzbekistan,
2019–2023,5 which supports inclusive and sustainable economic growth through financial market
development, access to finance, private sector development, and domestic resource mobilization.
The additional financing supports key operational priorities of ADB’s Strategy 2030 such as (i)
addressing remaining poverty and reducing inequalities through broader access to housing
finance which will contribute to improving living standards; (ii) accelerating progress in gender
equality and women’s empowerment through women’s enhanced access to mortgage loans; (iii)
tackling climate change through enhancing environmental sustainability by introducing a green
renovation loan product; and (iv) strengthening the governance and institutional capacity of
UzMRC.6

5.      The additional financing will increase the volume of subloans refinanced by UzMRC to
13,000, of which 800 will be for green renovation loans under output 3. The percentage of the
ADB loan portfolio that targets women will increase from 30.0% to 32.5%. The new activities under
the additional financing will be completed by 2025. The additional financing modality is
appropriate 7 since the added activities supplement actions of the original FIL. The original
project’s modality, design, and implementation arrangements can also be used efficiently.

B.      Program Description

6.     The program is aligned with the following impact: living standards improved for the
population of Uzbekistan.8 The program will have the following outcome: availability of affordable
market-based residential mortgage credit increased. 9 The program’s outputs are (i) policy,
regulatory, and legal framework for the mortgage finance industry strengthened; (ii) housing
finance strategy and subsidy framework strengthened; and (iii) the establishment and
operationalization of the mortgage refinancing company supported with long-term funding. The
impact, outcome, and outputs will remain unchanged for the program.

7.      Under output 3 of the original project, UzMRC was established with charter capital of
SUM25 billion by the sole shareholder, the Ministry of Finance (MOF), in November 2019. UzMRC
was partially privatized as 12 PFIs became shareholders in August 2021. As of 1 April 2022,
UzMRC has equity of SUM100 billion with 13 shareholders. The MOF remains the largest
shareholder, with a 25% share in UzMRC. The corporate governance framework and internal
controls, as well as the environmental and social management framework, are acceptable to ADB.
This includes the full spectrum of internal policies and procedures—including lending operations,
financial management, treasury operations, and risk management—to carry out refinancing
operations that UzMRC fully adopted in 2020. UzMRC and 12 PFIs signed the master refinance
agreement, which sets out the terms and conditions for accessing UzMRC’s refinancing facilities.

8.       With the additional financing being channeled through output 3, the project will expand
activities that comprise the provision of long-term funding to UzMRC to make long-term local
currency resources available to identified eligible PFIs to fund (i) residential mortgage loans,
(ii) housing improvement loans, and (iii) green renovation loans (a new loan product). Additionally,
the percentage of ADB-funded loan portfolio provided to women will increase from 30.0% to
32.5% (by value), and the volume of subloans refinanced by UzMRC will rise to 13,000, of which
800 subloans will be for green renovation and 4,225 for women. The green renovation loans will
be for eligible heating and cooling systems, solar roofing, water heaters, doors, and windows to
help support climate change mitigation. UzMRC will also operationalize the green renovation loan
tool kit (which includes an eligibility assessment of green renovation products, energy-saving
calculations, and loan eligibility certificate) to standardize the appraisal of green renovation loan
projects, establish information and technology systems that will be populated with sex-
disaggregated data for gender analysis, and adopt a 5-year business plan that will include
strategies to increase the share of lending to women. Individual consultants and a national survey
firm will be engaged through a change in scope of ADB’s ongoing technical assistance for the
Implementing the Mortgage Market Sector Development Program, which has an uncommitted
and available balance of approximately $304,000 to assist in these endeavors.10

9.      As a result of the expanded scope of output 3, the program’s original outcome indicator
targets will change: (i) the share of market-based mortgage loans to total mortgage loans of all
banks will increase from 40% to 90%, and (ii) the average initial tenor of market-based mortgage
loans will lengthen from 10 years to 15 years.

C.      Value Added by ADB

10.     ADB will continue to broaden access to housing finance across Uzbekistan through the
provision of critical long-term funding to PFIs. The attainment of a critical mass of mortgage loans
will ensure UzMRC’s sustainability, act as a catalyst for changes to enabling legislation on
covered bonds, and help increase the depth of the Uzbek financial markets through the
introduction of new financial products (mortgage bonds). The introduction of a green renovation
loan will promote awareness of household energy efficiency. The promotion of financing
mechanisms to catalyze market-based mortgage finance in Uzbekistan, expand the availability of
residential mortgage products including green financing, and bring product innovation to the
banking sector will also lengthen the tenor of market-based mortgage loans, all representing key
value additions by ADB. ADB’s contribution toward the establishment and operationalization of
UzMRC on a sustainable basis is unique and differentiates it from the services of other multilateral
development banks in the housing sector, which are focused on complementary activities such
as strengthening regulations for building housing codes and upgrading the real property
registration and cadaster system.

E.      Implementation Arrangements

15.     The program’s implementation arrangements will essentially be retained. The MOF (the
representative of the borrower and the program executing agency for the PBL) remains
responsible for overall coordination of the program and the additional financing.13 UzMRC (the
project executing agency for the FIL) will continue to be the executing agency for the additional
financing.14 UzMRC’s functions will be expanded to be consistent with the project’s upgraded
gender category of effective gender mainstreaming and its role in catalyzing the green renovation
loan product for subborrowers with the PFIs. Individual consultants and a national survey firm will
be engaged under the technical assistance for Implementing the Mortgage Market Sector
Development Program, which has an uncommitted and available balance of $304,000 to assist
UzMRC in these endeavors (footnote 10). Retroactive financing will be allowed for up to 20% of
the FIL loan amount for eligible mortgage subloans, disbursed before loan effectiveness but no
earlier than 12 months before the signing of the loan agreement. The implementation
arrangements are described in detail in the PAM (footnote 11). The implementation period is for
3 years from November 2022 to December 2025.

Key Activities with Milestones
3. The establishment and operationalization of the mortgage refinancing company supported
     with long-term funding.
3.1. Prepare the UzMRC’s organizational chart (completed).
3.2. Hire senior management (e.g., chief executive and financial officers) and key employees
     (e.g., internal auditor and risk manager) (completed).
3.3. Draft master loan agreement governing the lending operations of the UzMRC with PFIs (completed).
3.4. Prepare and obtain UzMRC board approval for its internal and operational policies (completed).
3.5. Provide up to $150 $300 million for qualified UzMRC subloans by ADB (ongoing).
3.6. Implement the Green Renovation Loan Toolkit by UzMRC and the PFIs (by June 2023).
3.7. Operationalize information and technology systems fully by UzMRC (by December 2024).
3.8. Prepare and obtain UzMRC board approval for its strategic business plan internal and operational
     policies (by December 2025).
Project Management Activities: not applicable
