I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING

1. This Restructuring Paper seeks approval of a Level 2 restructuring of the Financial Inclusion for Households and
   MSMEs in the WAEMU Project (P181251) to revise the design of selected Performance-Based Conditions (PBCs)
   and improve disbursement arrangements, while maintaining the original Project Development Objective (PDO).
   The World Bank received a request for restructuring from the West African Development Bank (Banque Ouest
   Africaine de Developpement, BOAD) following the Project effectiveness and first supervision discussions held on
   March 2026.
2. The proposed restructuring aims to (i) better align the timing and allocation of disbursements under Performance-
   Based Condition 1 (PBC#1) with evolving market conditions and the expected pipeline of CRRH-UEMOA bond
   issuances; and (ii) enhance operational efficiency in the management of project funds. Accordingly, the restructuring
   includes the following changes: (i) a revision of PBC#1 (which is the only PBC of the project); and (ii) an adjustment
   to disbursement arrangements to allow the opening of an additional segregated Designated Account for Component
   3.
   A. Overall Project Status

3. The Financial Inclusion for Households and MSMEs in the WAEMU Project (P181251) was approved on May 21,
   2025, and became effective on April 1, 2026. The Project is financed through IDA Credits 7804-3W, 7805-3W, and
   7806-3W, for a total amount of US$205 million.
4. Since effectiveness, implementation has focused on establishing operational arrangements, including fiduciary and
   institutional set-up, and advancing pipeline development under both the MSME finance and housing finance
   components. Initial progress has been made in operationalizing the Project, including coordination between BOAD
   and the West African Regional Mortgage Refinancing Company (Caisse Régionale de Refinancement Hypothécaire de
   l'UEMOA, CRRH-UEMOA) for the implementation of Component 3. Early implementation experience, including
   discussions during the recent supervision mission, has highlighted the need to refine certain aspects of the Project’s
   disbursement-linked structure and disbursement arrangements to better align with market conditions and
   operational requirements.
   B. Rationale for Restructuring
 5. Following project approval and during the early implementation period, consultations with BOAD have identified
    two operational adjustments to better align the Project with market conditions and enhance the efficiency of
    fund management. These changes are operational in nature and do not affect the Project Development Objective
    (PDO), scope, or overall financing envelope.
 6. Specifically, the proposed Level 2 restructuring includes:
     (a) Amendment to Performance-Based Condition 1 (PBC#1): PBC#1, which supports the mobilization of private
         capital through CRRH-UEMOA social bond issuances, requires adjustment to reflect the actual size and
         sequencing of issuances. At appraisal, PBC#1 was structured around two social bond issuances of broadly similar
         size. Since then, CRRH-UEMOA completed the first issuance that was substantially larger than originally
         anticipated and refined its future issuance pipeline based on market conditions and implementation experience.
         The proposed amendment revises the timing and disbursement allocations associated with PBC#1 to better
         align with the updated issuance program while preserving the overall expected results and total allocation of
         the PBC.
     (b) Modification of Disbursement Arrangements — Third Segregated Designated Account for Component 3: BOAD
         has requested the opening of a third segregated Designated Account (DA) specifically for funds allocated to

 II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES

    C. Change 1: Amendment to Performance-Based Conditions (PBCs)
  7. Current Arrangement: Component 2 (Housing Finance) includes PBC#1, which supports the mobilization of private
     capital through CRRH-UEMOA social bond issuances. At appraisal, PBC#1 was structured around two social bond
     issuances of at least US$60 million each, expected to take place during FY2026 and FY2027. The PBC allocation of
     EUR 37 million was therefore split equally across two tranches of EUR 18.5 million, with disbursements linked to the
     successful issuance of each bond. This structure reflected the appraisal assumption that CRRH-UEMOA would
     mobilize private capital through two bond issuances of similar size over the implementation period.
  8. Proposed Change: Since appraisal, CRRH-UEMOA completed a first social bond issuance of FCFA 60 billion
     (approximately US$100 million), significantly exceeding the size originally anticipated for the first issuance of US$60
     million. In addition, CRRH-UEMOA has refined its future issuance pipeline based on market conditions and the
     higher-than-expected amount of the first issuance proceeds. The CRRH- UEMOA still intends to issue a second bond.
     Accordingly, Schedule 4 (Performance-Based Conditions) of the Financing Agreement will be amended to reflect the
     revised timing, issuance amounts, and associated disbursement allocations of the two tranches under PBC#1. The
     total PBC allocation remains unchanged at EUR 37 million and continues to support the same objective of mobilizing
     private capital through CRRH-UEMOA social bond issuances. Additionally, the proceed amounts were originally
     denominated in US dollars and these will now be reflected in the local currency issuance of FCFA.
  9. The tables below show the original and revised structures (note: the bond issuance thresholds are expressed in the

    10. Changes:
        (a) The reference periods shift from World Bank fiscal years (FY2026–FY2027) to client calendar years (CY2025–
            CY2026), reflecting that CRRH-UEMOA's first social bond issuance took place in 2025;
        (b) The bond issuance thresholds are revised from the original appraisal assumption of two social bonds of at
            least US$60 million each to reflect the actual and planned size and sequencing of CRRH-UEMOA's social bond
            issuances. Tranche 1 is linked to a social bond issuance of at least FCFA 60 billion (approximately US$100
            million equivalent), which exceeds the original appraisal assumption. Tranche 2 is linked to an additional social
            bond issuance of at least FCFA 20 billion. The revised structure maintains the total PBC allocation and overall
            objective of mobilizing private capital. The new overall expected issuances for both PBCs stands at FCFA 80
            billion which exceeds the originally planned amount of US$120 million equivalent to FCFA 70 billion.
        (c) The disbursement split is revised from equal tranches (EUR 18.5 million each) to recognize the results of the
            first issuance and to front load the additional liquidity towards housing loans structure (EUR 27.75 million for
            Tranche 1 and EUR 9.25 million for Tranche 2), consistent with the larger bond issuance already completed in
            2025. The total allocation of EUR 37 million remains unchanged and overall project results are also unchanged.
        (d) Financing Agreement Amendment: Schedule 4 (Performance-Based Conditions) and Section III.A (Withdrawal
            of Proceeds) of Schedule 2 of the Financing Agreement will be amended accordingly.
    D. Change 2: Opening of a Dedicated Designated Account for Component 3

    11. Current Arrangement: Project implementation support expenditures under Component 3 are implemented and
        managed by BOAD. Under the current disbursement arrangements, expenditures for Component 3 are
        processed through the existing Designated Accounts established for the Project and do not benefit from a
        dedicated account for implementation support activities.
    12. Proposed Change: BOAD has requested the establishment of a third segregated Designated Account for
        Component 3, Category 5 (IDA Credit No. 7806-3W), to be opened at Banque Atlantique-Togo instead of the
        BCEAO. The account will be used exclusively for project implementation support expenditures under Component
        3, which is implemented by BOAD. A dedicated account will facilitate more efficient management, monitoring,
        and reporting of implementation support expenditures: A third segregated Designated Account will be opened
        specifically for Component 3, Category 5 of the Project (IDA Credit No. 7806-3W). Key features of this account are:
