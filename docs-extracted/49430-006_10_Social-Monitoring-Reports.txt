 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

 1.     This social monitoring report covers the period from January to December 2025 for
 Tranche 1 of the Aimags and Soums Green Regional Development Investment Program (ASDIP),
 financed by the Asian Development Bank (ADB), the Green Climate Fund, and the European
 Investment Bank. This consolidated annual report replaces the two semiannual reports for 2025,
 as social safeguard monitoring activities were conducted throughout the full year. The report is
 prepared in compliance with ADB's Safeguard Policy Statement (2009) and the Loan Agreement
 between ADB and the Government of Mongolia.

 2.     ASDIP is classified as Category B for Involuntary Resettlement and Category C for
 Indigenous Peoples (IP) under ADB's Safeguard Policy Statement. The project remained in the
 pre-construction phase throughout the reporting period. No land acquisition, physical
 displacement, or compensation payments were carried out. No resettlement-related grievances
 were received.

 3.      Key resettlement-related activities during the reporting period include (i) field visits to the
 three target aimags (Khovd, Bayan-Ulgii, Uvs) in May 2025 to assess land use conditions and
 consult with local authorities on land acquisition issues; (ii) coordination with aimag and soum
 governments on land documentation and dispute resolution; (iii) grievance redress mechanism
 registration in the government Enterprise Resource Planning (government integrated system) (ERP)
 system; (iv) identification of key land-related issues; and (v) designation of alternative Agro-Park
 sites in both Uvs and Bayan-Ulgii aimags through Citizens' Representative Khural (ITKh)
 resolutions in December 2025, resolving the site suitability issue identified during field missions.

I. PROJECT INTRODUCTION

A. Purpose of the Report

4.      This consolidated annual social monitoring report describes and assesses the status of
involuntary resettlement and indigenous peoples safeguard compliance for ASDIP Tranche 1
during January–December 2025. This report consolidates the two semiannual monitoring periods
(January–June and July–December 2025) into a single annual report, as the project remained in
the pre-construction phase throughout the year and no resettlement activities were implemented.

5.      This social monitoring report has been submitted in compliance with ADB's Safeguard
Policy Statement (2009)1 and the Loan Agreement between ADB and the Government of
Mongolia2. The report describes (i) resettlement safeguard and Indigenous Peoples compliance
status; (ii) resettlement plan (RP) and resettlement framework implementation status; (iii) land
acquisition and affected persons status; (iv) stakeholder consultations related to land and
resettlement; (v) grievance redress mechanism (GRM) status and grievances received; and (vi)
planned actions for the next reporting period.

B.       Background of the Project

6.       ASDIP will promote local economic development and improve basic urban services in
priority aimags of Mongolia3. The program supports the government in establishing inclusive,
green urban development, resilient rangeland management, and sustainable agribusiness value
chains in select aimags. Tranche 1 covers the three western aimag centers of Ulaangom (Uvs
Aimag), Ulgii (Bayan-Ulgii Aimag), and Jargalant (Khovd Aimag), as well as four soum centers
(Omnogovi and Tarialan in Uvs Aimag, and Delüün and Buyant in Bayan-Ulgii Aimag). The project
is implemented under a multitranche financing facility over a 10-year period (2023–2033) with
three tranches. Tranche 1 commenced in 2024 and is expected to be completed by 2029. The
total program cost is estimated at $735 million, co-financed by ADB, the Green Climate Fund
(administered by ADB), and the European Investment Bank.

7.       The four outputs of ASDIP are (i) Output 1: Climate-resilient, low-carbon, and attractive
aimag and soum centers developed; (ii) Output 2: Climate-resilient, high-carbon sequestration,
and sustainable rangeland and agricultural management implemented; (iii) Output 3: Accessible
financing for low-carbon and climate-resilient livestock and agribusiness value chains created;
and (iv) Output 4: Institutional capacity and policies for low-carbon and climate-resilient agro-
territorial development strengthened. Land acquisition and resettlement impacts under Tranche 1
are associated with Output 1 urban infrastructure investments in Uvs and Bayan-Ulgii aimag
centers. The project is classified as Category B for Involuntary Resettlement under ADB's
Safeguard Policy Statement. A Resettlement Framework has been prepared to guide screening,
impact assessment, and preparation of RPs. A revised resettlement plan for Tranche 1 was
prepared based on feasibility design. The Tranche 1 RP covers Uvs and Bayan-Ulgii aimags only.
No land acquisition or resettlement impacts are expected in Khovd Aimag under Tranche 1.4

II.     RESETTLEMENT SAFEGUARD COMPLIANCE

A.       Resettlement Framework and Resettlement Plan

10.    The resettlement framework was prepared during project preparation to guide screening,
impact assessment, and RP preparation in accordance with ADB's Safeguard Policy Statement
and national legal requirements. The resettlement framework has been reviewed and approved by
ADB and disclosed on the ADB project website in February 2023.5

11.      A revised RP was prepared for Tranche 1 based on feasibility design. According to the
preliminary assessment, the project may affect 26 residential households and three business
entities, with a total affected land area of 1,936 square meters (m²) across 21 land parcels (20
partially affected, 1 fully affected). There are 84 potentially affected persons (45 male, 39 female)
in 26 households. The three affected organizations comprise one fuel station, one wholesale
business, and one government social welfare organization. The RP has been disclosed on the
ADB project website.6

12.     The RP will be revised and updated upon completion of detailed design by the detailed
design and supervision (DDS) consulting team and submitted to ADB for review and concurrence
prior to the commencement of any land acquisition activities or award of civil works contracts. As
of December 2025, the DDS consulting team has not yet been mobilized; the request for proposals
has been issued to shortlisted firms and contract award is pending.

IV.      INDIGENOUS PEOPLES SAFEGUARD STATUS

A.      Categorization

19.     Tranche 1 of the ASDIP Investment Program is classified as Category C for impacts on
Indigenous Peoples in accordance with ADB's Safeguard Policy Statement, Safeguard
Requirement 3 (SR-3). The social assessment conducted during project preparation did not
establish that any of the affected ethnic groups in the three targeted aimags (Bayan-Ulgii, Khovd,
and Uvs) meet the criteria for classification as Indigenous Peoples under ADB's Safeguard Policy
Statement SR-3. Accordingly, no Indigenous Peoples Plan (IPP) is required for Tranche 1, and
the Indigenous Peoples safeguards are not triggered.

B.      Ethnic Groups in the Project Area

20.     The three targeted aimags are home to a diverse range of ethnic groups. Kazakh people
constitute the predominant ethnic group in Bayan-Ulgii (86.3% of aimag population), while Uvs
Aimag is primarily composed of Durvud (42.7%) and Bayad (34.1%) populations. Khovd Aimag
hosts the most ethnically diverse population, including Khalkh (27.1%), Zakhchin (24.5%), Kazakh
(12.1%), and several other groups.

21.     While the Kazakh and Tuva ethnic groups present cultural and linguistic distinctions from
the Khalkh majority — including distinct language, religion (Islam), and cultural practices — the
TRTA social assessment concluded that their livelihoods are not significantly differentiated from
other ethnic groups in the project area. No group was found to meet the full set of SR-3 criteria,
particularly with respect to collective attachment to geographically distinct ancestral territories and
the presence of customary cultural, economic, social, or political institutions separate from those
of the dominant society.

C.      IPPF Status

22.    The Indigenous Peoples Planning Framework (IPPF)7, prepared in February 2023,
remains in place to guide IPP preparation for subsequent tranches should any future tranche be
categorized A or B for Indigenous Peoples impacts. The IPPF establishes screening procedures,
IPP preparation steps, consultation and disclosure requirements, grievance redress
arrangements, and monitoring protocols in accordance with ADB Safeguard Policy Statement SR-
3.

23.     No issues related to Indigenous Peoples impacts were identified during the January–
December 2025 reporting period. No complaints or grievances related to ethnic minority concerns
were received. Community engagement activities were carried out in a culturally appropriate and
inclusive manner, with outreach materials and consultations accessible to all ethnic groups in the
project area, including Kazakh-speaking communities in Bayan-Ulgii. In Bayan-Ulgii, Kazakh-
speaking PIU staff facilitated consultations to ensure meaningful participation by the Kazakh-
majority population.

24.   The IPPF categorization screening will be conducted as part of project preparation for
subsequent tranches. If a future tranche is categorized A or B for Indigenous Peoples impacts,

30.   The GRM will be introduced to communities during follow-up consultations and will be
made publicly available at the three aimag and four soum centers.

31.   The GRM covers complaints related to land acquisition, resettlement, and other social
safeguard issues from affected persons.

32.    The GRM operates through three escalation stages: Stage 1 (maximum 7 working days,
aimag PIU level); Stage 2 (maximum 15 working days, PIU ERP system); Stage 3 (maximum 15
working days, Ministry of Economy and Development [MED] level).

33.     No grievances related to land acquisition, resettlement, or other social safeguard matters
were received during January–December 2025. The GRM operates through up to three escalation
stages as described below.

B.        GRM Stages and Timeframe

34.       Stage 1 (maximum 7 working days): If a concern arises, the affected person may raise the


                                                                                                                   13
ASDIP —Social Monitoring Report (January–December 2025) | Project No. 49430-005



issue with the contractor, PIU-GRM staff at the aimag PIU, project implementation and
management support consultants, or community representatives. All agencies will immediately
report any incidents to the UB PIU GRM staff. The log/record shall be saved in the GRM logbook
at the PIU office.

35.     Stage 2 (maximum 15 working days): If the issue is not resolved at the aimag field office,
the complaint is forwarded to the UB PIU ERP system, where the relevant Agency for Land
Management and Administration, Geodesy, and Cartography or the National Federation of
Pasture User Groups and/or the Ministry of Food, Agriculture and Light Industry intervene. A
written record of the whole process is maintained.

36.      Stage 3 (maximum 15 working days): If the issue is still not resolved, the PIU will, if agreed
by the affected person, arrange a meeting with MED and relevant community representatives. If
still unresolved, the issue will be referred to relevant higher-level authorities. MED with PIU
support, may report to ADB at any stage but will do so immediately if Stage 3 is reached.

37.   All affected persons retain the right to pursue legal remedies through Mongolia's court
system at any time, independently of the project GRM.

VII.     CONCLUSION AND PLANNED ACTIONS

38.      The project complied with all applicable resettlement safeguard requirements during the
reporting period. The project remained in the pre-construction phase with no land acquisition,
physical displacement, or compensation payments carried out. Key land-related issues have been
identified and are being addressed through coordination with local authorities. The alternative
Agro-Park sites in Omnogovi Soum (Uvs) and Ulgii Soum (Bayan-Ulgii) were formally resolved
through ITKh resolutions in December 2025.

39.     The following actions are planned for the next reporting period (January–June 2026):
        (i)     Update the RP upon mobilization of the DDS consulting team and completion of
                detailed design; submit to ADB for review and concurrence prior to any land
                acquisition.
        (ii)    Conduct formal consultations with potentially affected persons during the RP
                update process.
        (iii)   Update the RP to reflect confirmed alternative Agro-Park sites in Omnogovi
                Soum (Uvs) and Ulgii Soum (Bayan-Ulgii) upon DDS mobilization.

                certificate processing for project beneficiaries.

                mission to Khovd, Bayan-Ulgii, and Uvs aimags, planned for Q2 2026 in
                conjunction with DDS1 mobilization activities.
        (vi)    Initiate the recruitment of the external social safeguards experts within the 2026
                period.
        (vii)   Conduct capacity-building training for aimag and soum-level PIU officers and
                local representatives on safeguard requirements, RP update process, and GRM
                procedures in parallel with the detailed engineering design process.
        (viii) Submit the semiannual social monitoring report covering January–June 2026 to
                ADB.
